Two years ago, I weighed 280 pounds and couldn't run a city block.

I didn't need a better app. I didn't need a coach. I needed a commitment small enough that I couldn't negotiate my way out of it.

Seven minutes. Every day. Bodyweight movement in my living room.

That commitment compounded into 80 pounds lost, a Boston Marathon finish, an Ironman 70.3, and an MBA at MIT Sloan.

Today I'm launching Jerome7 -- and I want to share why.

During my time at MIT (Sloan + the AI Studio at the Media Lab), I watched brilliant builders collapse under the weight of productivity culture. Not from laziness. From caring about too many things and never learning to show up for themselves first.

The data is stark: 86% of Gen Z report burnout before 25. Fitness app retention at 30 days is 4%. The industry keeps adding features. We need less.

Jerome7 generates one 7-minute session per day. Same session for every person on Earth. 7 blocks, 60 seconds each. Bodyweight only. No equipment. No decisions.

Like Wordle, but you move.

Under the hood, 5 AI agents coordinate to keep builders consistent:

- A Coach that adapts from your session feedback
- A Nudge system that predicts when you'll skip and intervenes before you break your chain
- A 3-miss chain rule (because binary streaks are psychologically fragile)
- Accountability pods of 3-5 matched by timezone and level
- An AI voice that narrates each block live

The technical innovation I'm most proud of: Jerome7 is MCP-native. It's a Model Context Protocol server that any AI assistant can call natively. Your AI becomes your accountability partner through standard tool calls. This is what the agent ecosystem should look like -- not walled gardens, but composable capabilities.

There's a live 3D globe at jerome7.com/globe where every builder who shows up becomes a dot. Watching it light up across time zones is the most rewarding thing I've built.

Jerome7 is:
- Personally funded by the founder. No premium tier. No ads.
- Open source under MIT
- Available from your terminal: npx jerome7

I'm personally investing $1,000 into this community, tracked publicly. Because this isn't a startup. It's a commitment to the builders who don't have time to take care of themselves but need it the most.

I met my co-founder Miguel through a cold LinkedIn message. One person. That's all it took. Cold plunge baths, saunas, sessions together. YU are one person away from someone who will change the trajectory of your life.
